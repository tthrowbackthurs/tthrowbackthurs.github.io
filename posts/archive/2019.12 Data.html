Historical Background

Though the idea of religion as we know it has only existed since the late 1500’s, belief systems, particularly in a higher being, likely date back to the advent of language. From the time that humans were able to tell stories, especially as they pertained to morality and civilized living, belief systems became part of the identity for people within localized groups.

Over time, as civilizations modernized and nations formed, the beliefs of the individuals slowly melded into more organized belief systems. Ancient civilizations are well known for their religious ideas – from the Egyptians, to the Greeks and Romans, to the Mesopotamians and Jews, as well as the Hindus and Buddhism – their beliefs were a cornerstone of their civilization.

Over time, major events have caused shifts in religious beliefs among people. Examples include the move to a monotheistic worship of the God Aten by Egyptian Pharaoh Akhenaten, or the rise of Christianity in the first century CE.

More recently, there have been shifts within religions. As modern ideals, or needs, find themselves out of alignment with traditional teachings, some religions have seen decreases or increases in their followers. Recently, the Catholic Church has seen a recommendation to lift a roughly 1,000 year ban on married Priests to combat a shortage of candidates in the Amazon region. The concept of “adapt or die” is not new, but regardless, this move has been seen by some as counter to the church’s values, and could lead to changes in the number of followers.
