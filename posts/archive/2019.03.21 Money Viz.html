DataViz Approach

For this week's dataset, I had a couple of ideas that immediately came to mind. The first, that I didn't go with was creating a stacked unit chart that looked like a stack of bills and coins. I had some issues getting it to look just right, so I decided to go with my other idea, using images of currency to indicate the percentage of production by denomination. I'm really enjoying small multiples lately, so I decided to throw that in too.

First, I decided that I wanted to be able to use the form factor for the different types of currency - a bar chart makes sense for the bills and a pie makes sense for coins (even though I typically despise pie charts). Both charts are constructed in very similar methods - they both rely on dual axis charts where one of the axes is a shape, and they both rely on percent of total calculated fields. Quick table calcs for percent of total wouldn't work in this situation, so I created some level of details to calculate total production by denomination type (bill or coin) then divided the production for my selected denomination by that.

Enough of the calcs...Well, maybe not so quick. For the bar/bill viz, I wanted to white out (with some tranparency) the percentage of production that was not tied to the selected denomination. This was easy, I just subtracted 1 from my calculated Percent of Total, and fixed the axis to make it align properly to the bill. Unfortunately, Tableau makes creating a pie chart super easy, but doesn't allow you to leave a slice transparent. So I had to do a bit of trial and error to get my colors just right on the coins. With some patience, the right shades and transparency, they pop just like I was hoping.

For the dashboards, I wanted these to be unique depending on whether the viewer was viewing coins or bills. I could have forced this with some parameter driven sheet swapping, but I'm really liking the new dashboard buttons that came out with 2018.3. So, instead I created two very similar dashboards, and that was it.
