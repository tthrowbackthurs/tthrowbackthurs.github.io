Historical Background

We could go into how diamonds are formned, but the most important note from the process is that diamonds are pure carbon - the same element that makes up charcoal, pencil lead (graphite) and is present in all living things. The thing that sets diamonds apart from all other carbon based items is the perfect conditions under which they're formed.

Diamonds are thought to have first been mined and treasured in India over 3,000 years ago. Beyond being treasured for their gem beauty, they have also been used for engraving due to their trademark hardness. Due to improvements in mining techniques and the resulting increase in supply, the popularity of diamonds increased in the 19th century.

In the late 1800's, many of the smaller diamond mines were acquired by the founders of what would become De Beers. The marketing done by De Beers in the early 20th century is credited by many with creating the tradition of the engagement ring. Other conspiracy theories propose that De Beers (among others) control supply to create scarcity and increase the value of the gems - break out the tinfoil hats...
