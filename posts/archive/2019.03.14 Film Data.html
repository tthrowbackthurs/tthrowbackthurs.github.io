Historical Background

Film, or motion picture, traces its root back to the late 19th century in France when the Lumiere brothers debuted a series of ten short films. These early films were less than a minute in length and had no sound. Before "The Jazz Singer," the first 'talkie' or movie with synchonized dialogue and singing, was released in 1927, film would undergo several significant transformations. Arguably, none of these changes were as impactful as the addition of dialog to the films.

The movie "Singin' in the Rain" chronicles a fictionalized take on this change, but does a great job of conveying the excitement and feeling that surrounded the release of the the early talkies. Since this time, the film industry has seen one innovation after another - from feature length animated films to color to early special effects and more - the financial incentive to innovate and be at the top of the game is an incredible driver.

Our dataset this week contains gross box office statistics and tickets sold, broken out by genre from 1995 to 2018. Over this time, the cinematic world saw breatkthroughs like "Avatar", 8 different "Star Wars" films, the rise of Pixar and more. Though not at the film level, there are bound to be some interesting insights in the data.
