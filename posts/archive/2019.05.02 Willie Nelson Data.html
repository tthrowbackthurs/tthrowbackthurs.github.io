Historical Background

Yesterday, April 29 was Willie Nelson's 86th birthday. At 86, Willie is still going strong as ever - touring and releasing new music. Willie's first album was released in 1962, over 56 years ago. Since that time, he has released 147 albums (including compilations and a couple soundtracks that he was the primary artist on). However, Willie got his start before that first album.

Willie got his start in the late 1950's as a songwriter, writing songs that are still played to this day in the honky tonks of Nashville. One of his early successes was "Crazy", popularized by Patsy Cline when her rendition went to #2 on the Country Singles Chart, and #9 on the all genres chart. This and other successes, including Roy Orbison's rendition of "Pretty Paper," helped provide Nelson the opportunity to record on his own.

His no-nonsense approach, long braids, well worn guitar "Trigger" and his iconic sound have helped to elevate Willie Nelson into the pantheon of Country Music greats. During his 60 plus years in the business, Willie has had the opportunity to rub shoulders and share memories with several others in that esteemed group as well - including Kris Kristofferson, Waylon Jennings, Merle Haggard and Johnny Cash among others.
