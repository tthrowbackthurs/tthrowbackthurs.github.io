Data Storytelling and Dataviz Approach

A couple weeks ago, I came across Ken Flerlage's post on creating a customizable curvy timeline in Tableau. Since then, I've been waiting for the right dataset to take his methodology and put my spin on it.

This week's dataset was the perfect excuse to use the timeline. I won't go into the details on building the base timeline because Ken did a great job of it on his site - check it out here. I created all of the calculated fields that Ken describes, as well as a couple others to fit how I wated to present the timeline. The viz uses a dual axis where the first is a line graph, using a calculated field combining the year and point to create the path.

The second axis uses the shapes mark. Doing this allowed me to highlight the single point that I wanted to show for the year. If you've read Ken's blog, you'll know that for each year there are 20 points, so this was important for how I wanted to visualize the data. To hide the other points, I used a blank shape (a 1px x 1px png with a transparent background) for the points I didn't want to show and years that Poe did not publish any works. The dual axis also allowed me to have two different labels - one for the year, and a second showing the number of works published by Poe in that year. Both of these are driven by calculations to only show at my desired points.

In addition to driving the label, the [Number of Works] calculation is also responsible for the size and color of the circle on the timeline. The timeline is a pretty simple look, but I wanted to be able to provide a bit more background on the works for each year. To accomplish this, I used two different Viz in Tooltips to provide the additional data. The first is a break out of works by type filtered to the year the user is hovering over. The second is a listing of the works for that year.

The resulting look is really simple, but the visualization is quite nice. Another week's viz that was a lot of fun.
